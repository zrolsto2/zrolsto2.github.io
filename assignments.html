Presentation
Due: April 27th

General Guidelines:
  • Length requirement: strictly between 18 and 20 minutes.
  • Everyone in the group must speak in the presentation for at least 2 minutes (unless otherwise cleared beforehand with the professor.)
  • Each group's presentation must utilize some form of electronic media. However, the use of videos is prohibited. Using a video will result in a significant reduction in the group's grade.
  • Each member of the group will receive the same grade. However, if you do not contribute to your group, you can lose points individually.
  • Each presentation should use at least 5 scholarly sources (no wikipedia) your presentation must include a citations slide other form of media. Citations must be in Chicago or MLA format.
  • If you are unsure about anything about the presentation, you can come by the professors office hours to discuss it.

Research Paper 
Due: May 5th

Each student must complete a paper of no less than 5 pages and no more than 7 pages . 

The topic for the paper will be an analysis of the state and local election process. It can cover elections during any of the historical periods we have discussed, or the current election. You must discuss candidates, candidate and party platforms, election results, controversies, initiatives on the ballot, as well as social and political ramifications at the local, state, and national levels. 

Grade Break Down:  

  Content : 70 Points. Sources must be scholarly, appropriate, and factual. MSNBC, Fox News, and Wikipedia cannot be used. You will lose 5 points per citation if you violate this policy. Citations must be noted in the text. You may use footnotes for citations. Your paper must be well organized, with clear separation between sections. Organization and structure will account for 20 points of your total content. Your paper must include a thesis statement which is worth 5 points. Your paper must be written in formal academic style, do not use personal pronouns (using I, you, or we). Your paper should be written in the 3rd person point of view. You may conduct personal interviews for your paper but you must follow proper Chicago or MLA formatting for conducting and citing interviews. 

You must use paragraphs of no less than 4 sentences. Your paper should be factual, present complete arguments and avoid bias. Failure to present proper content in your paper will result in a loss of points in relation to size of, or frequency of the mistakes. 

Grammar : 10 Points. You will lose points for mistakes such as spelling, punctuation, sentence structure, etc. Each mistake will incur a penalty of 1 point. You can lose a maximum of 10 points from grammatical mistakes. 

Citation Page : 10 Points. Citations must be in MLA or Chicago format. You will lose 1 point for mistakes in citation format up to a maximum of 10 points. 

Formatting : 10 Points. Your paper must be in 12 point, times new Roman or Liberation Serif (for Linux users) font. 5-7 pages double spaced. This does not include your cover or citations page. I want 5-7 pages of content. 1 inch margins are required. You must include a cover page which includes your name, Blinn ID number, section, class start time, date, and Govt 2306. Failure to do any of these things will result in 10 points being being deducted. 

I highly suggest taking your paper by the writing center either on campus at Blinn or at A&M. 

I am available to answer questions regarding the paper by e-mail, appointment or during office hours. 

I am willing to look over papers ahead of time for content analysis. 

Papers are due by 5:00 p.m. on May 5th . You can turn in your papers ahead of time. Papers will be penalized 25 points per day that they are late. You must turn in a paper copy to me unless you have arranged ahead of time with me to have it submitted electronically. I will only accept electronic copies under special circumstances.
